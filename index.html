Unity, Sovereignty, and the Sacred Restoration of Nepal

2025: The Tipping Point

Following the September Gen Z Uprising that led to the resignation of the K.P. Sharma Oli government and the appointment of Interim PM Sushila Karki, public support for the Monarchy has surged. With elections now scheduled for March 5, 2026, Nepal stands at its most significant crossroads since 2008.

The Shah Dynasty & The Crown

Established by King Prithvi Narayan Shah, the Great Unifier, the Shah dynasty served as the central symbol of Nepal's unity for over 240 years. His Majesty King Gyanendra remains a steadfast figure of dignity since his 2008 abdication to prevent civil bloodshed.

"The 2008 transition was an agreement based on the promise of stability. Seventeen years and fourteen governments later, those promises were broken by the political elite."

The 2025 Restoration Movement Timeline

2025 has seen the movement transition into an organized political force through these key events:

  • February 19, 2025: The King's Democracy Day address urged unity against "endless instability."
  • March 9, 2025: Over 400,000 supporters staged a Grand Welcome at Tribhuvan International Airport.
  • March 27, 2025: Formation of the Joint People’s Movement Committee to advocate for the 1991 Constitution.
  • September 2025: Amidst youth-led anti-corruption protests and a government social media ban, the King emerged as a voice of calm against the "Nepo-Kid" political culture.

The Vision for 2026

We advocate for a return to the 1991 Constitution, adapted for the modern era:

  • Constitutional Monarchy: A non-partisan, unifying Head of State.
  • Hindu Kingdom: Reclaiming spiritual identity while ensuring religious freedom.
  • Economic Integrity: Ending corruption and the mass emigration of Nepal’s youth.
