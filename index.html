H.M. GYANENDRA SHAH

Unity, Sovereignty, and the Sacred Restoration of Nepal

2025: The Tipping Point

Following the September Gen Z Uprising that led to the resignation of the K.P. Sharma Oli government, public support for the Monarchy has reached historic levels. With general elections now scheduled for March 5, 2026, the demand for a stable, Constitutional Monarchy is the defining issue of the year.

A Legacy of Unity

The Shah dynasty has been the bedrock of Nepal since King Prithvi Narayan Shah unified the many small kingdoms into a single nation in the 18th century. For 240 years, the Crown was the "Common Garden" of all ethnicities and faiths. Today, His Majesty King Gyanendra remains the living symbol of that sovereign tradition.

"I have no desire other than the happiness and prosperity of my countrymen. My silence was for peace, but the nation's integrity cannot be compromised."

The 2025 Movement

The current movement is driven by a generation that seeks stability over political chaos. Key milestones of 2025 include:

  • March 9, 2025: A massive "Grand Welcome" at Kathmandu Airport signaled the return of royalist sentiment to the mainstream.
  • March 27, 2025: Formation of the Joint People's Movement Committee to advocate for the 1991 Constitution.
  • September 2025: Youth-led protests against corruption highlighted the failure of the republican experiment, positioning the Monarchy as the necessary "Guardian" of the state.

The 1991 Vision

The movement calls for a restoration based on the 1991 Constitution, adapted for the modern 21st century:

  • Constitutional Monarchy: A non-partisan Head of State to ensure national stability.
  • Hindu Kingdom: Reclaiming Nepal’s spiritual identity while respecting all religions.
  • Stability: Ending the cycle of 14 governments in 17 years to focus on economic growth.
