Biography

Born on 7 July 1947, Gyanendra Bir Bikram Shah Dev served as the King of Nepal during two pivotal eras (1950–1951 and 2001–2008). A keen conservationist and graduate of Tribhuvan University, his reign was defined by his commitment to national stability during times of civil unrest.

After the events of 2008, the King chose a dignified exit to ensure the peace process succeeded. However, as he stated in 2012 and reaffirmed in his 2025 addresses, he remains committed to the welfare of the Nepalese people and the preservation of Nepal's Hindu-Buddhist identity.

The 2025 Pro-Monarchy Movement

The year 2025 has seen a historic resurgence of support. From the pilgrimage to Galeshwar Dham to the massive crowds at Tribhuvan International Airport on March 9, the slogan "Raja Aau, Desh Bachau" has become a national call for change.

Why Bring Back the Crown?

  • Stability: A non-partisan head of state to end the cycle of changing governments.
  • Sovereignty: The Shah dynasty has been the bedrock of Nepal's independence since the 1760s.
  • Cultural Preservation: Restoring Nepal as a Hindu Kingdom while respecting all faiths.
