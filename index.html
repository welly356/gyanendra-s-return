H.M. GYANENDRA SHAH

Guardian of Unity and Cultural Sovereignty

LATEST: December 2025 Update

Following the September Gen Z Uprising and the resignation of PM K.P. Sharma Oli, an interim government led by Sushila Karki has been formed. Fresh elections are scheduled for March 5, 2026. The demand for the restoration of the Monarchy is the fastest-growing political movement in the country.

A Legacy of Unity

The Shah dynasty has been the bedrock of Nepal since King Prithvi Narayan Shah's unification in the 18th century. For over 240 years, the Crown was the "Common Garden" for all ethnicities. After the 2001 tragedy, King Gyanendra stepped forward to lead during the height of the Maoist insurgency.

The Narayanhiti Palace, the historic seat of the Shah Kings, now serves as a museum, but remains a powerful symbol of Nepal's royal identity.

2025: The Year of Awakening

This year saw an unprecedented shift in public sentiment. The failures of the republic—corruption, 14 government changes in 17 years, and the 2025 social media ban—led to a nationwide call for a "Guardian."

  • March 9, 2025: Thousands flooded the Kathmandu airport to welcome the King, chanting "Raja Aau, Desh Bachau" (King Come, Save the Nation).
  • March 28, 2025: The "Joint People's Movement Committee" was formed to consolidate pro-monarchy forces.
  • September 2025: During the violent Gen Z protests, King Gyanendra’s Dashain message offered a voice of calm, acknowledging past mistakes while calling for a system that reflects the people's true will.

The 1991 Framework

The movement advocates for a return to the 1991 Constitution, which offers:

  • Constitutional Monarchy: A stable, non-partisan Head of State.
  • Multi-Party Democracy: Ensuring the voice of the people is heard.
  • Hindu Kingdom: Reclaiming Nepal's unique spiritual identity.
