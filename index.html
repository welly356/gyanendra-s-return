Unity, Sovereignty, and the Sacred Restoration of Nepal

2025: The Tipping Point

Following the September Gen Z Uprising that led to the resignation of the K.P. Sharma Oli government and the appointment of the Interim Administration under Sushila Karki, public support for the Monarchy has surged. With general elections now scheduled for March 5, 2026, Nepal stands at its most significant crossroads since 2008.

The Shah Dynasty & The Crown

Rooted in the mid-18th century, the Shah dynasty was established by King Prithvi Narayan Shah, the Great Unifier. For over 240 years, the monarchy served as the central symbol of Nepal's unity and cultural identity. His Majesty King Gyanendra, the last reigning monarch, has remained a steadfast figure of dignity since his voluntary abdication in 2008 to prevent civil bloodshed.

"The 2008 transition was an agreement based on the promise of stability and prosperity. Seventeen years and fourteen governments later, it is clear that those promises were broken by the political elite."

The 2025 Restoration Movement

2025 has been the year the silence was broken. The movement has transitioned from nostalgia to an organized political force through these key events:

  • February 19, 2025: The King's Democracy Day address urged unity, stating that "the people are tired of endless instability."
  • March 9, 2025: Over 10,000 supporters (estimates suggest up to 400,000) staged a Grand Welcome at Tribhuvan International Airport, the largest royalist gathering in decades.
  • March 27, 2025: Formation of the Joint People’s Movement Committee to formally advocate for the reinstatement of the 1991 Constitution.
  • September 2025: Amidst youth-led anti-corruption protests and a government social media ban, the King emerged as a rare voice of calm, validating the grievances of the "Gen Z" protesters against the "Nepo-Kid" political culture.

The Vision for 2026

We advocate for a return to the 1991 Constitution, adapted for the modern era. This vision includes:

  • Constitutional Monarchy: A non-partisan, unifying Head of State to provide stability.
  • Hindu Kingdom: Reclaiming Nepal’s spiritual identity while ensuring religious freedom for all.
  • Economic Integrity: An end to the corruption and frequent government changes that have left Nepal’s youth with no choice but to seek work abroad.
